Resume

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at University College London (UCL), where I develop curriculum learning and world model methods that enable embodied agents to learn from synthetic data and generalize to real-world environments. I am currently also a Research Scientist Intern at Odyssey, working on world models and multi-agent interaction. I am a member of the BOLD Lab (formerly UCL DARK Lab), and I am co-supervised by Ilija Bogunovic and Jack Parker-Holder. I am also affiliated with the Rhine AI Lab. Before starting my PhD at UCL, I completed a year-long Deep Learning Research Internship at Huawei R&D in Cambridge, UK, and received an MSc in Artificial Intelligence from the University of Leeds. My research focuses on world models, embodied AI, open-ended learning, curriculum learning, reinforcement learning, and multi-agent systems, with an emphasis on agents that learn efficiently from imagined experience and generalize to complex environments. Prior to academia, I worked in motorsport engineering, but my long-term goal has always been building machines that can think rather than race.
